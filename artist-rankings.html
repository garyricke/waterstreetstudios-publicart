One reviewer's read of the seventeen artist applications received for the America 250 City Hall Mural and the unWIND River Street Plaza Benches. Ranked favorite to least favorite, intended as a starting point for committee discussion — not a final selection.

Project One · 8 Submissions · 1 Selection

America 250
City Hall Mural

A permanent six-panel mural across the arched limestone bays of Batavia City Hall. $9,650 artist fee · Illinois artists only · Illinois Arts Council grant.

01

Artist 2 · "Batavia: The Currents of America"

Six-panel bold-linework narrative tying Big Woods to Fermilab.

Grew up in Geneva Deep public-art portfolio Illinois-based

A right-to-left visual sequence: Emerging from The Big Woods (Judge Wilson, 1833) → The Westward Hub (covered-wagon manufacturing) → The Windmill City → The Social Fabric (Flag Day ceremony, Lincoln family) → Frontier of the Small (Fermilab) → The Living Current (today's Riverwalk). Painted in a vibrant, bold-linework illustrative style with the Fox River threading through every panel as a literal current.

The single strongest balance across every criterion. Concept is editorial, original, and directly responsive to the "Living Community Story" prompt — each panel earns its place. Portfolio is the deepest of the eight: Wrigleyville "Bleacher Bums," Chicago Academy HS "Learner's Life" (with student-engagement process), the Illinois Art-in-Architecture finalist Monarch Youth Center "Transform," plus Illinois Dept. of Health editorial work on gun-violence healing. Has a defined production schedule (nighttime mapping, daylight execution, anti-graffiti seal) and the local connection — raised in Geneva — is real, not performed.
Six fully illustrated panels in a 7–10 day cadence is ambitious. Worth confirming whether their schedule allows the longer June–August window we've reserved, and whether community-input touchpoints (Batavia Historical Society "Making of the Mural Day" they proposed) can be scheduled in May.
02

Artist 1 · Six-panel flowing-flag visual journey

Sepia-to-vibrant color progression unified by a Declaration-of-Independence flag motif.

Chicago-based 25+ regional murals Painted "Art on the Fox" in Batavia, 2024

Opens with a Bernard J. Cigrand portrait and a flowing American flag layered with handwritten Declaration text — that flag becomes the through-line tying all six panels together. Panels progress from sepia (windmills, railroad expansion) to fully saturated color (Boardwalk Shops, a child gazing forward, the Peace on Earth bridge with fireworks). Proposes QR codes alongside each panel for deeper Batavia Historical Society content.

Genuinely original device — the flag-as-connective-tissue solves the "six separate frames" problem without making the panels feel repetitive. Portfolio is the most directly comparable to what we're commissioning: the Heartland (St. Charles), the Hive (St. Charles), Misfits Jiu Jitsu, and the Green Mill / Uptown Jazz mural in Chicago all show his ability to deliver large-scale exterior work at this fee. Provides dimensioned sketches and notes the BHS collaboration upfront.
Style runs vibrant-pop — some jury members may prefer something more muted against limestone. Pop-art portraiture (Bowie/Basquiat) reads strongly on canvas but jury will want to see how a Cigrand portrait at this scale lands in the architectural context.
03

Artist 3 · "Batavia at 250: A Living Community Story"

Photorealistic six-panel mural with hometown roots.

Raised in Batavia · Batavia schools Bradley University BFA Painted at Art of the Fox arched bay

Cinematic, illustrative-realism panels: Cigrand by the Fox with native wildlife → the Windmill City → a folded-flag presentation honoring service → prairie/bison heritage → a young artist painting the Batavia Bulldog at the Riverwalk bandshell → children gazing across the river at Fermilab with the Vulpecula (Little Fox) constellation overhead. Civic navy / cream / gold palette.

Strongest hometown-connection narrative of the eight: attended Batavia schools, names the teachers who influenced him, his Great Blue Heron mural for the Conservation Foundation already sits inside an arched limestone bay — he has literally painted this site condition before. The presentation document itself is the most polished. Cigrand+fox panel and the Vulpecula constellation closer are the most visually memorable single-panel ideas in the pool.
The concept studies are clearly AI-rendered, which introduces an execution-risk gap between the polished proposal and what hand-painted realism actually delivers at site scale. Six photorealistic panels in the timeline we have is the heaviest lift of any proposal — worth a direct conversation about whether the final design will simplify, and what hand-painted reference work he can show at scale.
04

Artist 4 · "Community Hands Mural"

Hands across history holding flags — designed as a companion to the Flag Day Monument.

Aurora-based Brief WSS studio resident, 2021 Lead artist, "Love Aurora" (15′×30′)

Six panels of hands holding flags: community hands holding the Batavia flag (sourced from resident photos), an American flag surrounded by native birds, Chief Shabbona's hand with a Potawatomi flag (pending tribal-government approval), John Ozier with a Civil-War-era flag, women's hands stitching a flag c.1818, and children's hands with America 250 ribbon flags. Bright blue-aqua background, realistic skin tones across panels.

Most thoughtfully built community-engagement plan of the pool — she's explicit that she'll solicit hand-reference photos from Batavia residents and will pursue tribal-government approval for the Shabbona panel, which is the kind of cultural care this project genuinely needs. The "companion to the Flag Day Monument" framing is the cleanest theme-tie any applicant made. Detailed itemized $9,650 budget that lands exactly at our fee.
The current preliminary sketch reads less ambitious than her own portfolio work — "Love Aurora" has more visual punch than the proposed grayscale sketch suggests. Jury will want to see a fuller color treatment of at least one panel before deciding.
05

Artist 8 · Single-image submission

"Our Past. Our Present. Our Future. Together."

No portfolio, statement, or budget submitted

A polished six-panel mural rendered in full color across the City Hall facade: Roots (Native American figure) → Foundations → Industry → Community → Peace Bridge (Peace on Earth signage) → Stewardship (Fox River). Anchored by the tagline "One River. One Community. One Nation." Editorial-grade composition; clearly the most thought-through single-image submission.

If we ranked on the visual mockup alone, this would land in the top three — the panel titles, the tagline, the figure work, and the color cohesion are all strong. Hits every theme beat the brief asked for.
Submitted as a single image with no artist statement, no portfolio, no budget, no community-engagement plan, no schedule, and no proof-of-Illinois-residency for the grant requirement. We can't evaluate feasibility or experience without that context. Worth reaching out to ask whether a full application is forthcoming before we close jurying.
06

Artist 6 · Late-19th-century-realism six-panel proposal

Forty-plus-year career, Washington-Crossing-the-Delaware reference palette.

40+ years professional 37-mural Waves of DuPage commission Currently commissioned by Cleve Carney for America 250

Six panels reading right-to-left: a river crossing (courage / resilience) → the railroad → the Windmill City → Flag Day & Cigrand → the Fox River → vibrant present-day Batavia. Stated style reference is Emanuel Leutze's Washington Crossing the Delaware.

Easily the most credentialed applicant by résumé: the Cleve Carney Museum of Art has already commissioned this artist for five ten-foot sculptural heads opening June 2026 for America's 250th — meaning their current studio practice is literally on this exact theme. Chinese American Museum of Chicago mural, DuPage Children's Museum, Orvis stained-glass installations nationwide. Proven reliability at scale.

The proposal text itself is the thinnest in the top half of the pool — one short paragraph per section, no preliminary panel sketches, just a stated style reference. For a $9,650 grant-funded civic mural, jury should expect more developed concept work. Also: the Washington-Crossing realist register may read as derivative of national symbolism rather than as a Batavia-specific story.
07

Artist 7 · Flat-color geometric six-panel timeline

Minimalist landscape-to-Fermilab narrative in muted earth tones.

Sycamore, IL · NIU BA 2016 Limited large-scale public-art history

Geometric, flat-color panels: open landscape → settlement (with windmill) → Fox River → Flag Day Monument → Depot Museum → Fermilab. Limited warm-earth / muted-neutral palette intended to complement the limestone.

The most architecturally restrained proposal — if the committee wants something that defers to the limestone rather than competes with it, this is the candidate. Color palette is the most considered against the building. Concept is internally coherent.
Demonstrated mural experience is one indoor floral mural in a Geneva private residence plus assistant work on the iFLY Skydiving mural in Schaumburg. That's a meaningful gap for a $9,650 six-panel civic exterior commission. Jury would be betting on capacity rather than confirming it.
08

Artist 5 · Native flowers + Batavia landmarks

Illinois-flora panels overlaid with translucent Batavia architecture.

Three years muralist Chicagoland nonprofit teaching artist

Two concept variants: (a) 3D-textured Illinois native flowers (echinacea, coneflower, monarda) overlapping Batavia landmarks like the Public Library and church spires; (b) the same landmarks layered with a translucent Flag-Day-stars-and-stripes overlay. Itemized $3,488 budget with Nova water-resistant paint.

Sincere social-impact mural practice — in-house muralist for Young Chicago Authors and the Breathing Room Collective, plus City of Aurora public-art work. The flora-over-landmark device has potential. Comes in well under our $9,650 fee.
The concept doesn't engage the America 250 / "Living Community Story" theme as directly as competitors — flowers in front of a library reads as decoration rather than narrative. Three years of muralist experience and a 2,127–3,488 budget range suggests the leap to a six-panel grant-funded civic mural is a stretch — she may be a better fit for the unWIND commission (where she also applied, see below).

America 250 · Summary recommendation

Bring Artist 2, Artist 1, and Artist 3 into the committee discussion. If Artist 8 submits a complete application before jury close, they belong in the conversation as well.

Project Two · 9 Submissions · 3 Selections

unWIND
Benches

Three painted Noblewins benches — two Arch and one Arcadia — for River Street Plaza. $3,200 per artist · on-site painting June 5–15.

Heads-up on bench-style mismatch: Several applicants proposed designs on the Noblewins Acadia sail-form bench, which Noblewins discontinued in Fall 2025. The brief calls for two Arch and one Arcadia. Confirm with each Acadia-targeted artist whether they can adapt to the actual Arcadia geometry before final selection.

01

Artist 8 · Quilted-patchwork Arch bench

Americana quilt blocks with a windmill, stars, strawberries, and a folk-art bird.

Graphic designer / illustrator / painter Polished site templates included

A signature warm palette (chartreuse, olive, burnt orange, lavender, dusty blue, cream) applied as a quilt-block patchwork wrapping the Arch bench — each tile a tiny piece of local iconography (windmill, harvest stars, strawberries, dandelions, a folk-art bird). Includes a mounted-hardware template overlay showing exactly where the design avoids the bench-foot anchors.

Best fit for the plaza brief of all nine: explicitly "welcoming, community-focused," directly responsive to the copper-toned leaf privacy fencing without literally repeating it, and the patchwork format gives the plaza visitor something to discover up close. The template page alone signals the level of professional preparation we want for a public installation. Portfolio (Little Barn Baby, Where Beautiful Things Come Together, You Are Abundantly Loved) shows consistent palette discipline at mural scale.
Style runs sweet/whimsical — pairs well with one of the three benches but might feel monotonal if all three commissions land in the same register. Best treated as one of the two Arch slots.
02

Artist 7 · Three benches as a unified set

Neutrinos. Windmills. Dodecahedrons. A rambling, layered visual system across the plaza.

30 years studio + administrative arts career Commissioned by Kai Japanese Kitchen, Cross Street Apts (Aurora)

The only artist offering a coherent three-bench system rather than a single design. Three sketch directions presented as wraps: Neutrinos (blue/yellow Fermilab-coded swirls), Windmills (silhouettes against orange sky), Dodecahedrons (colorful faceted polyhedra). Layered, improvisational mark-making style.

If the committee is at all worried about three benches feeling visually unrelated when installed together, this is the candidate who solves that problem — one artist, three concepts, one identifiable hand. The Cross Street Apartments dodecahedron piece (Aurora 2025) is real and at scale. The Neutrinos direction is the strongest single-bench Fermilab nod in the entire pool.
Sketches are presented as rectangles, not yet wrapped to the bench geometry — we'd need to see at least one mock-up on the actual Arch or Arcadia form before committing. Also: this depends on us deciding whether we want visual unity across three benches, or whether we'd rather see three distinct voices.
03

Artist 6 · Falling-leaves Arch bench

Autumn canopy wrapped around the Arch — a direct dialogue with the copper privacy fencing.

Clean, polished render Site-aware concept statement

A bold, graphic field of oak, maple, and ash leaves in seasonal ochres / burnt oranges / vibrant ambers wrapping the Arch bench's full circumference. Statement frames it as "a visual bridge between Batavia's industrial heritage and Fox River ecosystem." $500 supplies + $2,500 design = $3,000.

The most explicit site-responsive design in the entire bench pool — the copper-toned leaf-patterned privacy fencing is named in our brief as a context element, and this is the only proposal that picks up that thread directly. Render quality is high; the bench mockup actually shows the design on the Arch geometry, not just floating illustration.
Light on portfolio context within the PDF itself — we have the concept render but not enough exterior-mural reference work to know how the painted execution will compare to the digital render. Worth requesting two or three previously completed exterior pieces before final selection.
04

Artist 4 · Underwater Fox River with windmill reflection

Native plants, lilypads, mallards, and a windmill shadow in the water.

Graphic designer / painter Habitat for Humanity "Buzzing Springs," 2025

Outer panels: native plants, flowing ripple patterns, the shadow of a windmill interacting with the river surface, scattered lilypads, mallards. Interior of the Arch: an underwater view (lilypad undersides, turtle, fish). Color palette explicitly tied to the plaza's copper privacy fencing (blues / greens / yellow / copper hints).

Strongest concept for site response in the pool — she's the only applicant who treated the inside-of-the-arch as a distinct design opportunity, which fits the "pause, gather, connect" brief beautifully. Inspiration page (windmill photo, river-ripple references) shows she went to the site.
Portfolio (Adirondack chair, embroidery hoop, acrylic clouds, Habitat for Humanity "Buzzing Springs") is mostly smaller-scale work — the leap to a 123 sq.ft. wrap is real. Concept sketches are stylized but rough; will want to see a more refined rendering before committing.
05

Artist 2 · Native flora & fauna in expressive painterly color

Echinacea, lavender, monarch, goldfinch, turtle — Fox River vitality in vibrant drips and washes.

Vibrant pink / orange / purple / teal palette Proposed for discontinued Acadia bench

A vibrant tribute to Batavia's native prairie ecology: echinacea, black-eyed Susans, lavender, painted goldfinch, monarch butterfly, native painted turtle. Painted in a loose, drippy, expressive painterly style on the sail-form bench.

Most expressive painterly hand of the bench pool — would bring a level of textural energy the plaza doesn't currently have. Native-flora-and-fauna framing is the most ecologically literate of the bench applications.
Proposed specifically for the Acadia sail-form bench — Noblewins discontinued the Acadia in Fall 2025. Worth a quick conversation about whether she'd adapt the same design language to the Arcadia (which is the actual tall sail-form bench we're commissioning).
06

Artist 1 · Photorealistic floral wrap on Acadia sail-form

Two design directions: orchid and blue Puya alpestris (Chilean blue lily).

$120K Sunset Blvd commission, multiple ArtsWA STEAM murals Proposed for discontinued Acadia bench Designs not Illinois-native

Two highly photorealistic floral concepts on the Acadia: a pink phalaenopsis-orchid wrap with banana leaves, and a blue Puya alpestris (Chilean blue lily) wrap. Pricing model: $35/sq.ft. × 106.5 sq.ft. = $3,710 (will deliver at our $3,200 cap).

Most credentialed muralist in the unWIND pool — "Grand Summation of Molecular Motion" (Sunset Blvd, West Hollywood, $120K), Katherine G. Johnson Elementary STEM murals (Tacoma, $33.5K each), the Bucktown floral wall in Chicago. Operates at a tier well above what $3,200 typically buys.
Same Acadia-discontinued issue as Artist 2, plus a concept-fit concern: phalaenopsis orchids and Chilean blue lilies are tropical / South American — they read as decorative wallpaper rather than as Batavia. The brief asked for designs reflecting "the beauty, diversity, and spirit of downtown Batavia," and these don't. Compelling artist; not the right concept for this plaza without revision.
07

Artist 3 · Illinois ponds, rivers, and prairie flora

Two-panel underwater + native-prairie wrap on the Noblewins arch.

In-house muralist, Young Chicago Authors / Breathing Room Collective ~3 years muralist

Left panel: an underwater Illinois pond scene with native fish, water lilies, river stones. Right panel: native Illinois prairie flowers (echinacea, monarda) and pollinators — "iconic plants we see growing in Illinois gardens." Budget $2,127.

Same artist as America 250 Artist 5, and the unWIND brief is a better fit for her current scale — her social-impact mural portfolio (#LetUsBreathe, Love Fridge, mariachi mural) shows she can deliver expressive work in public space. Genuinely under budget.
Concept sketches feel preliminary — the two arches are presented separately rather than as a single wrap-around design, and there's no rendering of the bench itself. The technical reading of the bench surface needs more work.
08

Artist 9 · Norman-Rockwell-inspired figurative scenes

Two warm narrative concepts — a boy fishing with a cat, and a multigenerational bench tableau.

Assisted on Naperville Loves a Parade mural (3 years) Pencil sketches only

Two figurative directions: (1) a boy catching a fish at the Fox while a cat watches and children look on — with a second composition of children approaching a garden gate; (2) a populated bench tableau (elderly woman with a dog, gentleman dozing under a newspaper, kids asleep with a teddy bear, a young couple). Stated style: impressionist watercolor brush-strokes letting light/white show through.

Of all the bench applications, this is the one that most directly answers the "pause, gather, connect" brief on its own terms — people sitting on the bench will see other people enjoying the bench. Warm, narrative, human.
Submitted as pencil sketches only — no color render, no portfolio piece at remotely comparable scale (assistant credit on the Naperville Century Walk mural plus small canvas portraits). Painted-figure realism on a curved bench surface in a 10-day on-site window is a heavy ask for an emerging artist.
09

Artist 5 · Photo-realistic windmills at dusk

"Bright cheerful windmills outside, relaxing dusk inside" — same 40-year career as America 250 Artist 6.

40+ years professional Stock photo references only, no painted concept

Concept presented as five stock-photo references showing windmills at varying times of day. Two-sentence written concept. $3,200 flat compensation including paint and materials.

Major credentials (Chinese American Museum, DuPage Children's Museum, Orvis nationwide). If we want a "safe veteran hand" on one of the benches, the underlying capability is real.
The submission itself is the thinnest of any of the seventeen total applications — no painted concept, no design sketch, no portfolio image specific to bench-scale work, no community-engagement plan. For a competitive jury process this is below the threshold of what we should fund.

unWIND · Summary recommendation

Strongest three-bench combination, if we want three distinct voices: Artist 8 (Arch · patchwork), Artist 6 (Arch · falling leaves), Artist 4 (Arcadia · underwater Fox River).

Alternative, if we want plaza-wide visual unity: commission Artist 7 for all three benches (Neutrinos + Windmills as the two Arches, Dodecahedrons as the Arcadia) and treat it as a single integrated public-art installation.

For Committee Discussion

Things worth surfacing as a group.

01

The Acadia / Arcadia naming confusion.

Three applicants (Artist 1, Artist 2, and the Noblewins spec sheets they used) reference the Acadia bench, which Noblewins discontinued in Fall 2025. Our brief calls for two Arch and one Arcadia. Before final selection, confirm with each artist whether they'd adapt their design to the Arcadia geometry, and consider clarifying the bench-model language in any future open call.

02

Artist 8's incomplete America 250 application.

The single-image submission with the "Our Past. Our Present. Our Future. Together." tagline is among the strongest visual concepts we received, but submitted without artist statement, portfolio, budget, residency proof, or community-engagement plan. Before the May 15 notification window closes, decide whether to (a) reach out and request a complete application, (b) treat it as withdrawn, or (c) keep it in the pool as a concept reference.

03

AI-rendered concepts vs. demonstrated hand-painting.

Artist 3 (America 250) submitted very polished but clearly AI-generated panel studies. The portfolio supports their hand-painting capability, but it's worth a direct conversation about how the realized mural will differ from the AI mock-ups — both to manage expectations and to lock in the actual painted style.

04

One artist, three benches — or three artists, three benches?

The brief assumes three artists, one each. Artist 7 is the only applicant to propose all three as a unified set. There's a real plaza-design tradeoff here: three artists deliver more community voice but risk visual disjointedness next to the metal tree iron and copper leaf fencing; one artist across three benches reads as a single installation but means we fund one voice rather than three. Worth deciding this before we make selections.

05

Cultural responsibility on the Shabbona panel.

Artist 4's "Community Hands" proposal includes a panel of Chief Shabbona holding the Potawatomi flag, and she's stated she will seek Citizen Potawatomi tribal-government approval. This is the right approach. If we move forward with her proposal — or with any proposal that includes Native imagery — we should formalize that tribal-consultation requirement into the artist contract rather than leaving it to the artist's initiative.
