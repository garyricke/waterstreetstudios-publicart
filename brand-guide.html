01

Identity

Full name

Water Street Studios Public Art Initiative

Use the full name on first reference in any document, press release, or formal communication.

Short form

the public art initiative

Use lowercase "the public art initiative" or "the initiative" in running text after first reference. Never use BPAI.

Deprecated

BPAI

Batavia Public Art Initiative (BPAI) is the former name. Do not use in any current communications, signage, or digital content.

Parent org

Water Street Studios

Legal entity: Batavia Artists Association NFP Inc, dba Water Street Studios. Use WSS as an abbreviation after first reference.

Contrast Rule — WCAG 2.1 AA

Normal text (<24px) requires 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Large text (≥24px bold or ≥18.67px) requires 3:1. The original WSS Blue (#009BC7) fails AA for normal text on white at 2.86:1. Always use #006B8F for any body-size text on a light background.

04

Voice & Tone

We write like a curator, not a committee. Editorial conviction over institutional caution.

Direct

We don't hedge or pad. Every sentence earns its place. "We are seeking proposals from Illinois-based artists" not "We are currently in the process of soliciting proposals from artists."

Civic, not corporate

We're rooted in Batavia. We name real places, real streets, real people. We don't write for a general audience — we write for this specific community.

Earnest, not trendy

No "vibrant," "passionate," or "fostering creativity." Say what actually happened: 76 volunteers painted a mural in seven days. That's the story.

Do

"City of Good Energy was painted by over 75 community volunteers alongside a project manager from September 6–13, 2025."

Don't

"We are passionate about fostering a vibrant creative community through innovative public art experiences."

Do

"We are seeking proposals" or "Submit a proposal"

Don't

"Apply Now" with a dollar amount as the primary CTA — implies a transaction, not an artistic invitation

Do — Referring to the initiative

First reference: "Water Street Studios Public Art Initiative"
After that: "the public art initiative" or "the initiative"

Don't

"BPAI" — deprecated. Never use this abbreviation in any current content, signage, or digital communication.

Button Rules

  • — All button text is ALL CAPS, JetBrains Mono, tracking-widest
  • — No border-radius. Square corners are intentional — they reference industrial precision.
  • — One primary action per section. Don't stack two black buttons side-by-side.
  • — Never put a dollar amount in a button label for artist calls.

09

Photography

Photography is the emotional core of this site. Every image should feel like it was taken by someone who cared about the moment — not staged for a brochure.

Do

  • — Real community members in candid moments
  • — High-res mural photography, full frame
  • — Wide establishing shots showing scale
  • — Natural light, outdoor on-site
  • — Architecture and texture: limestone, brick, Fox River
  • — Paint-day action: hands, brushes, ladders

Don't

  • — Stock photography of "generic artist" types
  • — People pointing at screens or clipboards
  • — Cropped murals that lose context
  • — Heavily filtered or over-edited images
  • — Low-resolution images (minimum 1200px wide for hero)
  • — Anything that doesn't feel like Batavia

Image Hosting

All images are hosted on Cloudinary with automatic format optimization (f_auto) and quality compression (q_auto). Always use the CDN URL pattern:

https://res.cloudinary.com/dsbllwpbh/image/upload/f_auto,q_auto/v{version}/{filename}

Generated placeholder images (nano-banana) must be uploaded to Cloudinary before any public deployment. Replace local file references with CDN URLs.

11

What to Avoid

Patterns that signal "AI-generated website" or "nonprofit template" — everything we are not.

Design Anti-Patterns

  • ❌ Centered hero with purple-to-blue gradient blob background
  • ❌ Three-column feature grids with generic line icons
  • ❌ Rounded corners on everything (cards, buttons, images)
  • ❌ Symmetrical, predictable section layouts
  • ❌ "AI startup" visual clichés (neural networks, sparkle icons, robot imagery)
  • ❌ Thin light-gray text on white backgrounds
  • ❌ Modal-heavy UX with long walls of small text
  • ❌ Generic sans-serif at uniform weight throughout

Content Anti-Patterns

  • ❌ "We are passionate about fostering vibrant creativity"
  • ❌ Dollar amounts as the primary lede in artist calls
  • ❌ "BPAI" in any form — deprecated brand, creates confusion
  • ❌ "Apply Now" on RFP calls — too transactional for artist recruitment
  • ❌ "1 business day response time" — sets a promise we may not keep
  • ❌ Past project stats presented as current (8 murals → was 10)
  • ❌ Pop-up gallery mentions — program is on indefinite pause
  • ❌ Crediting only unnamed "artists" — name Amy Henkle, Amy Decker
