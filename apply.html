Project Details

A permanent civic mural for Batavia City Hall.

A permanent outdoor mural funded by an Illinois Arts Council America's 250th Anniversary Public Art Grant. Six horizontal arched limestone bays will become civic "story frames" celebrating how Batavia residents live the ideals of the Declaration of Independence.

Theme — "America at 250: A Living Community Story"

The mural links local Batavia stories to the nation's founding principles, positioning America's 250th as a living, community-centered reflection on democracy. People are placed at the center, with imagery reflecting:

  • Learning and education
  • Environmental stewardship
  • Creativity and artistic expression
  • Civic engagement and shared governance
  • Everyday connection — life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness as experienced in Batavia today

Site

West-facing southern window wall along North Island Avenue, directly overlooking the Fox River corridor and the expanding Flag Day Monument grounds. The six recessed arched bays create naturally human-scale storytelling moments, with strong afternoon light ideal for layered imagery. All 6 window openings on the south-facing wall are confirmed for this project — including the larger opening on the left.

Compensation

$9,650 total artist fee, funded through the Illinois Arts Council's America 250 Creative Accelerator Fund. Site preparation, supplies, and equipment are provided separately by the public art initiative committee.

Eligibility

Open to Illinois-based artists or artist teams with experience in large-scale public artworks. Emerging and established artists welcome. Per grant requirements, artists must be Illinois-based.

2026 Timeline

  • Now – May 8: Open Call — applications accepted
  • May 8, 2026: Application deadline (11:59 PM CT)
  • May 9–15: Jury review & artist selection
  • Late May: Final design presentation and City Council approval
  • Jun–Jul: Surface preparation and painting
  • August: Painting completed
  • Late Summer: Public unveiling with municipal leaders, Batavia Historic Society, and Batavia Community Band

Selection

Submissions will be reviewed by a jury of City of Batavia representatives, property stakeholders, committee members, and arts professionals. No application fee. Questions? Email publicart@waterstreetstudios.org.

Project Details

Three artists. Three benches. One plaza on the river.

Three sculptural Noblewins benches — built from retired wind turbine blades upcycled into virtually indestructible public art — will be installed at the new River Street Plaza in downtown Batavia. Water Street Studios Public Art Initiative, in partnership with the City of Batavia, is commissioning three artists to paint them: two Arch benches and one Arcadia bench.

Theme — Connection Through Community

Each mural should reflect the beauty, diversity, and spirit of downtown Batavia while encouraging people to pause, gather, and connect. Designs should be community-focused and welcoming, incorporating graphic backgrounds and organic, flowing forms.

Site

River Street Plaza, 102 N. River Street, Batavia, IL — a vibrant new public gathering space that supports community events including the Batavia Farmers Market. Designs should complement existing site features: metal tree ironworks and copper-toned, leaf-patterned privacy fencing.

About the Benches

Noblewins crafts public-art benches from retired wind turbine blades — an industrial waste stream upcycled into permanent public art. Each bench arrives PRIMED with upcycled PVC board (mimics natural wood, won't warp or fade), an aluminum frame (rust- and saltwater-resistant), and 316 stainless steel fasteners. Backed by Noblewins' 25-year warranty. Bench specifications: noblewins.com/pages/resources.

Selected artists receive a paid contract of up to $3,200 covering final design development and on-site painting at the plaza (June 5–15). Final compensation reflects submitted scope.

Eligibility

Open to individual Illinois-based artists or artist teams — emerging or established. Mural, public art, or large-scale design experience is preferred but not required.

Submission Requirements

  • Artist statement (150–200 words)
  • Up to 3 design proposals — concept sketches or digital mock-ups, with brief written description and sample color palette
  • Portfolio — 3 to 5 examples of previous work
  • Proposed compensation — artist fee, plus any installation costs and materials

Selection Criteria

Submissions will be blind-juried by the Water Street Studios Public Art Initiative committee.

  • Artistic quality and originality
  • Alignment with the project theme
  • Feasibility and durability of design
  • Ability to complement the surrounding environment

Terms

  • Artists retain copyright of their designs; selected designs grant Water Street Studios and the City of Batavia usage rights for promotional and public display purposes.
  • Selected artists must provide proof of General Liability Insurance prior to project start.
  • The Public Art Initiative reserves the right to reject any or all proposals.
